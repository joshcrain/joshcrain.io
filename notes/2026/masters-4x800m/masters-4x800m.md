The [Drake Relays](https://godrakebulldogs.com/sports/drake-relays) is a track and field meet held in Des Moines. Billed as _America's Athletic Classic_, it is one of the top track and field events in the United States. It's also one of the oldest. I participated in the Relays in high school and then in college. Since then, I have returned each year to watch and spend time with friends.

Less than a week before the meet, I was contacted by two old high school teammates. Both planned to run the Masters 800m (40+) on Friday. They said they had been approached by race organizers who had added a new—and very last-minute—Masters event on Thursday: the 4x800m relay.

A group of Masters women wished to attempt the world record in the 65–69 age group. They reached out to the race coordinators, and the event was added to the schedule. However, to make the race official, they needed at least one other team on the track. Officials contacted Masters runners, including my friends, to see if they could assemble a team to help the ladies.

I agreed, and our team was added to the event. The following week, I was a little nervous. I would be anchoring, and I hadn’t been running regularly, so I worried about the pace. I got on the track a few days before to test it—and I was way off.

Then, before I knew it, it was race day. I received an email from the race organizer: the race needed to be moved up by two hours because of incoming weather. What had already been a little chaotic became a scramble, especially for those coming from out of town. Runners rushed to the stadium. One runner got the news while still on his plane.

But we all made it, and everything else went according to plan.

The team of Lesley Hinz, Deb Torneden, Julie Hayden, and Nancy Simmons [ran 11:28.28](https://results.drakerelays.us/?mid=8856&en=202-1), breaking the previous world record of 11:38.10.

What a thrill to be involved in this race. 

My hometown paper even wrote [an article](https://www.timesrepublican.com/sports/local-sports/2026/04/marshalltown-alumni-help-pace-world-record-in-4x800-relay/) about it.
